# RBI Agile Engineering Foundation Program for 42 Vienna

This program consists of three parts (learning blocks/ “Lerneinheiten”:
LE001, LE002, LE003.

The main objectives are:
- to provide students at 42 Vienna practical knowledge about agile SW-
  development processes in professional environments

- to give them minimum theoretical and more hands-on knowledge about SW-
  development processes in organizations

- to illustrate how students can utilize the coding skills and knowledge gained
  through the 42 curriculum (projects) to create professional products and
  services.

The parts are divided in several sessions (1 - 4) and it’s mandatory to participate in a
minimum number of sessions of LE001/002 to join the next parts LE002/003. Each
session lasts between 90 minutes and 4 hours.

Participating in this program will not be refunded in extra time/days for doing the
projects within the 42 curriculum.

The first part (LE001) is for BEGINNERS with no previous knowledge.

Registrations will be managed by 42 Vienna and not participating in a registered
session without a communicated reason results in an exclusion for the rest of the
program. Completing a short interview with RBI is a mandatory requirement for the
second and third parts (LE002/LE003) of this program.

Upon finishing each part, particularly after the third part (LE003), students who
successfully participate will receive a certificate from RBI, which can be used for
future job searches, career activities, and applications for various positions.
This program is the main additional training offered at the 42 Vienna Campus for
improving the knowledge and skills about SW-development processes.

All program parts will be done at the 42 Vienna Campus and/or RBI facilities. 42
Vienna Community Management will announce all details in advance via common
communication tools used at the Campus (42 intra and Discord).

## LE001 – Course:
Agile Engineering – Fundamentals
In this course, you will acquire fundamental knowledge of various Agile Development
topics, including:

### LE001.1: Agile SDLC
(Agile Software Development Life Cycle, Scrum, Extreme Programming)


### LE001.2: Agile Requirements Engineering
(Epics, Features, User Stories, etc.)

### LE001.3: Agile Testing
(e.g., Exploratory Testing, Session Best Testing, Testing Tours, Specifications by Example)

### LE001.4: DevOps
(Continuous Integration / Delivery / Development)

## Course content/ outcome
This fundamental knowledge is essential for every agile practitioner, forming the
basis for further learning within this program and the Agile Topics of the 42 Common
Core and Specialization Syllabus.
Each of these 4 theoretical topics will be presented through 90-minute lectures
without practical activities.
According to Blooms taxonomy these 4 topics cover knowledge level (K1)
remembering &amp; (K2) understanding.

By the end of this course, you will have a solid understanding of Agile
methodologies, effective requirements engineering, testing practices in Agile, and
the integration of DevOps principles into the Agile development process.

## Course details
### LE001.1: Agile SDLC (Agile, Scrum, Extreme Programming)

In this lecture, we will explore the Agile Software Development Life Cycle
(SDLC), covering well-known methodologies like Agile, Scrum and Extreme
Programming. Attendees will gain insights into the principles, values and
essential practices associated with each methodology.

Learning outcome:
 Understand the fundamentals of Agile methodologies.
 Differentiate between Scrum and Extreme Programming (XP).
 Identify key practices in Agile SDLC and XP.



### LE001.2: Agile Requirements Engineering (ARE)



Focusing on Agile Requirements Engineering, this lecture delves into effective
ways of gathering, managing and prioritizing requirements within Agile projects.
Participants will learn how to adapt traditional requirements engineering to the
iterative and collaborative nature of Agile methodologies.
 
Learning Outcome:
 Grasp the importance of flexible requirements in Agile (e.g., Epics, Features,
User Stories).
 Explore techniques for effective requirements elicitation and prioritization like
backlog grooming and story workshops.
 Understand how to maintain agility in changing requirements.
 

### LE001.3: Agile Testing (ATE)
In this lecture we will concentrate on the role of testing in Agile environments,
emphasizing continuous testing, acceptance test-driven development (ATDD)
and automated testing. Participants will gain insights into how testing practices
contribute to the overall success of Agile projects.

Learning Outcome:
 Comprehend the significance of testing in Agile development.
 Learn about Acceptance Test-Driven Development (ATDD) principles,
exploratory testing, testing tours, etc.
 Explore strategies for implementing automated testing in Agile.

### LE001.4: DevOps
The DevOps lecture covers the integration of development and operations to
enhance collaboration, automate processes and deliver high-quality software
faster.

Participants will understand the cultural and technical aspects of implementing
DevOps in Agile environments.

Learning Outcome:
 Grasp the principles and benefits of DevOps.
 Explore tools and practices for continuous integration and continuous delivery
(CI/CD).
 Understand the cultural shift required for successful DevOps adoption.

## LE002 - Course: Agile Engineering - Hands-On
Building upon the fundamental Part 1 (LE001 - Agile Engineering – Fundamentals)
of this program, this hands-on part allows you to apply your agile engineering
expertise in a real-world Scrum environment, further enhancing your skills within an
agile product setting.

### Course content / outcome
 Jointly with other participants you will be working as a Scrum team (Sprint ZERO)
with one Open-Source GitHub repository. During Sprint Zero, you lay the
groundwork and set up the necessary infrastructure for the project, ensuring that
future sprints have a clear focus on delivering value in an efficient manner. 
 In detail: as a team you will set up a Build Pipeline using GitHub Actions,
automating tests with Playwright while using agile engineering practices and
principles. You will also create and provision different environments (local,
staging, production) within this project.

### Course goal: prepare the organizational and technical foundation for Sprint 1 &amp; 2,
which will be used for Part 3 of this program - Scrum Project. The requirements to
setup this project will be described in a product backlog in form of Epics, User
Stories and Tasks.

## LE003 - Scrum Project (full practice part)
This third and last part (LE003) of the program, building upon LE001 (Agile
Engineering – Fundamentals) and LE002 (Agile Engineering - Hands-On),
provides you the opportunity to further deepen your understanding of agile
engineering within a real-world Scrum environment, enhancing your skills in an agile
product setting (Agile SDLC).

### Course content / outcome
 Throughout this project, you will collaborate with RBI-Coaches within an Agile
SDLC (Scrum) framework. Functioning as a Scrum team, you will be able to
implement the backlog items that were created during LE002 - Sprint Zero.

 Your primary goal will be to acquire the skills to prioritize backlog items, foster
effective collaboration, communication, and content alignment with key
stakeholders, including the product owner, scrum master and delivery lead. This
will ensure a shared understanding and expectations towards the product,
leading to a successful outcome for the joint product.

 Furthermore, you will have the opportunity to execute 2-3 sprints, fully
incorporating the mechanics of agile software methodologies and Scrum.
Throughout these sprints, you will engage in typical Scrum events such as Story
Elaboration Workshops, Backlog Grooming, Sprint Planning, Sprint Review, and
Sprint Retro.

 After finishing this Part 3 for the program, you will be able to have a good
understand of the agile framework, are able to have proper conversations with
different stakeholders and combine the agile methodology with your technical
background.
